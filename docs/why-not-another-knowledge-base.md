# Why Livebase is not another knowledge base

## The short answer

Because the project is not centered on storage.
It is centered on:

# keeping knowledge alive over time

---

## What most adjacent tools optimize for

Many adjacent tools optimize for one or more of these:
- capture
- editing
- organization
- linking
- search
- AI question answering

These are useful.
But they do not automatically solve:

- whether knowledge resurfaces when needed
- whether source truth survives abstraction
- whether related items keep staying related
- whether finished work strengthens the system
- whether the system gets better instead of noisier over time

---

## The sharper problem

The sharper problem is:

# knowledge decay after capture

A system can be excellent at capture and still poor at liveliness.
That is the distinction this project is trying to own.

---

## Bottom line

If `Livebase` becomes just another local note store, it has failed.
If it becomes a system that keeps knowledge callable, connected, and writable-back over time, then it becomes a distinct work.
